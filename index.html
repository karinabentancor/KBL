bio

Karina Bentancor Lenzi is a sound artist and designer based in Montevideo, Uruguay. Her practice spans sound installation, field recording, algorithmic composition, and theatrical sound design — moving between the technical and the poetic, between code and listening.

She holds a degree in Communication from Universidad de la República, graduating with Abstemia, a sonic fiction. She has trained across a wide range of practices: electroacoustic creation, modular synthesis, spatial audio, sound sculpture, and algorithmic systems — working with artists and researchers from Uruguay, Argentina, and Europe.

Her work has been presented at Festival Internacional de Arte Sonoro Monteaudio, Festival Sur Aural, and Radio Tsonami, among others. She has created sound installations, radio works, and site-specific interventions — often in collaboration, always in dialogue with space.

She teaches sound creation with SuperCollider at the Faculty of Information and Communication, Udelar, and has worked as assistant lecturer in Theatre Sound Space Design at the Escuela Multidisciplinaria de Arte Dramático.
